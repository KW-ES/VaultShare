Deep Dive

Market Risk / Systematic Risk


  • Market risk
    • also called systematic risk
  • is risk that affects
    • the entire market
    • or a large portion of it.
  • Source:
    • Comes from broad economic or financial factors.
Key Idea

You cannot eliminate it through diversification.

Typical sources

  • Interest rate changes
  • Inflation
  • Recessions or economic downturns
  • Geopolitical events
  • Market-wide sentiment shifts
Example
  • If global interest rates rise sharply,
  • most equities and bonds may fall.
    • Even a well-diversified portfolio would still be affected.

Example Risk Types

  • Interest rate risk
  • Inflation risk
  • Currency risk
  • Market crash risk

Specific Risk / Unsystematic Risk


  • Specific risk
    • also called unsystematic risk
    • or idiosyncratic risk
  • is risk unique to a particular
    • company,
    • industry,
    • or asset (product, security, equity, etc.)
Key Idea

You can reduce or eliminate it by diversification.

Typical sources

  • Poor company management
  • Product failures
  • Fraud or lawsuits
  • Industry-specific regulation
  • Supply chain disruptions
Example
  • If a single company’s CEO resigns unexpectedly
    • their stock might drop sharply.
  • But other companies may be unaffected.
